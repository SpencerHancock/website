Ballenas CubeSat Team

Thanks Gibson Clark, a former Ballenas graduate and Schulich Leader at the University of British Columbia, our high school was invited to build a 20cm by 20cm by 40cm cube satellite for the UBC rocket team's 10,000ft rocket. We settled on designing a satellite that tested the performance of five RaspberryPi computers in the extreme environment of launch. I was the software lead for the project and in order to measure the RaspberryPi's performances we had two computers take environmental data and others perform basic algebra, writing and reading from files on the hard-drives. Our plan was to compare the measurements of each computer as well as their performances using pre-built JupyterNotebooks but the rocket's parachute failed and we lost the bulk of our data.

Model United Nations

Throughout high school I had the fortune to participate in half a dozen or so Model United Nations conferences. The premise of the conferences is that you play the role of a given country and then navigate some predetermined event that requires action from all of the countries present at the conference. I am very proud of having been recognized at four conferences for my performances.

Rivers Oceans and Mountains School (ROAMS)

ROAMS was the opportunity of a lifetime. This one semester program taught a class of 20 students outdoor and first-aid skills fit for seasoned search and rescue teams. Thanks to ROAMS, I gained my OFA-3 ticket, became a certified national lifeguard, learned to surf and teach surfing lessons, got certified as a river raft guide and much much more. I owe ROAMS teachers Darin and Jake for teaching me to reach for the stars.

Vancouver Island School Ski Patrol Program

I was introduced to VISSPP via ROAMS, and throughout my senior high-school years, volunteered 30 or so days ski-patrolling on Mt. Washington and Mt. Cain. Becoming a volunteer member of Ski Patrol at Mt. Washington Alpine Resort is one of my proudest achievements.

Ballenas Interact Club

The Interact Club is a Rotary sponsored club of the school that encourages students to be leaders and have a positive impact on their community. In my senior year I was vice-president to Amanda Greenshields. I organized to have groups of students visit a local senior home on saturdays where we would play board games and more generally interact with them. My friend Gabriel Dodd and I also organized a community wide garage sale hwere we raised thousands of dollars to to help support the construciton of a new waterpark by parksville beach.
